About Me

Howdy! My name is Ryan Mok. I am born and raised in Lincolnwood, IL, which is a northern suburb of Chicago that nearly everyone has never heard of. I am currently enrolled in Northwestern University's full-stack coding program where I am learning how to become a web-developer. Aside from working full time and attending class, most of my free time is spent either being with my childhood friends, going to the gym, cooking, playing video games, or playing my saxophone.

I graduated from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2014 where I received my Bachelor's of Fine Arts in Music; I was and still am a fairly large music nerd. I was very active in the athletic bands program which included a Marching, Volleyball, and Basketball Band that performed at every home game; I joined mostly becasue a lot of my friends were also in them. University ensembles aside, I started an ad-hoc ensemble known as Sixth Street Brass. Most of our music can be classified as "riot-jazz", but we also wrote arrangements of popular music such as "No-Diggity" by Blackstreet (we found a way to make it work).

I look at the coding world as I would music; there's an order to it, there's a million ways to produce results, and there's always something new and refreshing being released to the public.
